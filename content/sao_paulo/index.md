description: "The year was 2018. Dani and I had already been supporting the English-language congregation for about one year. We didn’t know it at the time, but at a certain point that year, we would receive news that would completely change our lives within a few months. \n\n\ During 2018, a special meeting was organized where many brothers and sisters from various congregations—especially foreign-language congregations—were invited. At that meeting, we were informed about the international convention that would take place in Brazil in 2019, where we would host more than 7,000 brothers and sisters from over 13 countries. What an exciting announcement! It was quickly followed by a sense of anticipation as we prepared to organize a wonderful convention for the glory and honor of Jehovah. We immediately began the many preparations, but this convention would impact Dani and me even more than we imagined. \n\n\ Because of all the arrangements involved in organizing the international convention, we focused intensely on helping with as many activities as possible. However, my job was a limiting factor due to rotating shifts and long working hours. For some time, I had been thinking about becoming a regular pioneer, as I saw how happy Dani and the brothers in my congregation were in that service. That’s when Dani and I talked and prayed, and since we already had these plans, I decided to leave my job—where I had worked for more than 11 years—and begin full-time service, which allowed me to dedicate more time to convention activities. It was incredible to see how Jehovah blessed us for putting the Kingdom first in our lives. \n\n\ During this period, we devoted ourselves fully to convention activities. I began giving private English lessons to support us, while also studying software development at home. Remarkably, one of the assignments Jehovah allowed us to have was serving on a team welcoming convention delegates at the airport. The brother who was leading our team happened to own a software development company. Interestingly, during my first conversations with Brother Marcos, he was curious about what I did for work, and at that time I knew nothing about him. After a few conversations and explaining what I had done to support the convention and become a pioneer, Brother Marcos considered the possibility of talking about work after the convention—and that is exactly what happened. At the end of the activities, he invited me to work with him. \n\n\ This is just one of the many wonderful experiences we had the opportunity to live during that international convention that marked our lives. We decided to share this story because it became clear to us how Jehovah’s words at Matthew 6:33 became more real in our lives. It is evident that Jehovah’s hand is not short, and that He will always be by our side if we remain by His side. \n\n\ Next, you will be able to see a part of this story presented as a timeline through the following photos."
